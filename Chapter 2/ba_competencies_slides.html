BA Competencies Overview

Personal Qualities

How you think and interact with people around you. General skills important for any business environment.

Business Knowledge

Understanding of your organisation and the business domain within which it operates.

Professional Techniques

Specific to the business analyst role and differentiate BAs from other roles.

Key Insight: Behavioural skills are arguably more important than technical or business skills as they are a prerequisite for working with other people.

Personal Qualities: Communication

"Communication is perhaps the most important skill an individual can possess."

Key Communication Areas:

  • Building rapport - Establishing connection with stakeholders
  • Active listening - Understanding different perspectives
  • Influencing - Persuading and guiding decisions
  • Building empathy - Understanding others' viewpoints

Best Practices

  • Use language and style comfortable to business colleagues
  • Avoid unfamiliar terms and technical jargon
  • Understand business context through prior research
  • Adjust communication to align with participants
Slide 4 of 15

Other Critical Personal Qualities

Relationship Building

Getting people to share information and discuss ideas for change through genuine interest and mutual trust.

Influencing

Essential when recommendations are at odds with preconceived ideas or call for radical action.

Team Working

Collaborating with business colleagues, suppliers, project teams, and management effectively.

Political Awareness

Understanding what is politically acceptable and using the right organisational levers to get things done.

Analytical Skills & Critical Thinking

What Analytical Skills Mean in Practice:

  • Not settling for the obvious
  • Not accepting things at face value
  • Not jumping to premature conclusions
  • Digging deeper until the true situation is uncovered
  • Challenging received wisdom at every turn

Key Questions to Ask

Why do you do this? What value does it add? Where is it done? How is it done? Who is or should be responsible? When should it happen? Is there another way?

"Conduct 20% of the analysis to achieve 80% of the right answer - and then be 100% convincing when influencing the outcome."

Problem Solving & Leadership

Problem Solving

  • Understanding the problem before rushing to solutions
  • Curiosity and tenacity
  • Open mind that evaluates options
  • Pragmatic approach

Leadership in BA Context

  • Creating vision of approaches and options
  • Obtaining stakeholder agreement
  • Driving business and IT change process
  • Holistic thinking and rigorous analysis

Self-Belief & Professional Development

Self-belief: Having confidence in yourself, your analysis quality, and your approach to withstand pressure and sustain arguments.

Professional Development: Continuous improvement mindset through coaching, mentoring, training, and professional forums.

Business Knowledge: Finance

"The universal language of business is finance."

Essential Financial Knowledge:

  • Financial Statements: Balance sheet and income statement
  • Analysis Tools: Ratio analysis, budgeting, cash flow
  • Profit/Surplus: Understanding the nature of organizational returns
  • Costing: Principles of costing products and services

Business Case Development

BAs need to assess costs and benefits of projects, requiring understanding of:

  • Investment appraisal techniques
  • Break-even analysis
  • Discounted cash flow
  • Financial impact assessment
Slide 8 of 15

Domain & Technical Knowledge

Domain Knowledge

  • General understanding of business sector
  • Enables effective communication
  • Helps identify what's acceptable/useful
  • Allows application of best practices

Subject Matter Expertise

  • Specific product line or service knowledge
  • Detailed terminology and processes
  • Establishes credibility with customers
  • Identifies change opportunities

IT Principles (Bridging Role):

  • Computer systems fundamentals
  • Systems development lifecycles
  • Modelling approaches (UML)
  • Development methods (DSDM, Scrum)
  • Build vs. buy decisions
  • Emerging technology trends

Professional Techniques - Part 1

Project Management

Understanding PM processes, especially for small teams where BAs may take PM role.

Strategy Analysis

Techniques to understand business direction and organizational strengths/weaknesses.

Stakeholder Analysis & Management

Identifying, analyzing, and developing management strategies for stakeholders.

Investigation Techniques

Range of techniques to get to the root of business issues effectively.

Note: These techniques differentiate business analysts from other roles and are covered in detail in later chapters.

Slide 10 of 15

Professional Techniques - Part 2

Requirements Engineering

Practices leading to well-formed business requirements for solution development.

Business & Data Modelling

Visualizing business systems and analyzing data relationships.

Gap Analysis

Core BA skill - comparing 'as is' vs 'to be', evaluating packages against requirements.

Facilitation Skills

Workshop preparation and applying techniques like brainstorming, mind-mapping, Six Thinking Hats.

Emerging Areas:

  • Portfolio Management: Evaluation and prioritization of project portfolios
  • Benefits Management: Planning, monitoring, and evaluating predicted benefits
  • Agile Thinking: Supporting Agile projects and enabling business agility

How to Develop BA Skills

Training

Classroom-based learning for professional techniques, business knowledge, and personal skills. Industry qualifications available.

Self-Study

Reference books, Financial Times, Economist, Harvard Business Review, internet resources, specialist websites.

Workplace Experience

Best arena for developing personal skills. Enhanced through coaching, mentoring, and skills development programs.

Industry Engagement

Professional bodies (BCS, IIBA), conferences, certifications, networking, industry awards.

Skills Framework for the Information Age (SFIA)

SFIA Business Analysis Skill Definition

"The methodical investigation, analysis, review and documentation of all or part of a business in terms of business functions and processes, the information used and the data on which the information is based."

Level 1: Follow
Level 2: Assist
Level 3: Apply
Level 4: Enable
Level 5: Ensure, Advise
Level 6: Initiate, Influence
Level 7: Set Strategy

Related SFIA Skills:

  • Business process improvement
  • Stakeholder relationship management
  • Requirements definition and management

Key Takeaways

Evolution of the BA Role

From "collecting requirements" to broader skills that demonstrate value-add to organizations. Stakes are higher - BAs must show how they add value.

Critical Success Factors:

  • Personal Skills: Biggest challenge - overcoming change resistance and working effectively with stakeholders
  • Continuous Development: Most important aspect of career development
  • Organizational Context: Understand what your organization needs from BAs
  • Value Demonstration: Focus on identifying, analyzing, and developing value-adding options
"Business analysts can only survive and evolve if they offer a broad set of skills that demonstrate how they can identify, analyse and develop options for adding value to their organisation."
