What is Business Analysis?

Understanding the Discipline, Role, and Value

Chapter 1: Foundations of Business Analysis

Introduction & Purpose

  • Business Analysis has evolved over the last two decades
  • Ensures alignment between business needs and business change solutions
  • Solutions extend beyond IT systems to include:
    • Business process changes
    • Job role modifications
    • Organizational restructuring
  • Addresses the persistent lack of clarity about the BA role
  • Provides guidance reflecting the breadth and range of BA techniques

Key Problems in Business Analysis

  • Requirements Documentation Focus: Drive towards documenting requirements without clear understanding of desired business outcomes
  • IT Background Limitations: Former systems analysts less comfortable considering business requirements and solution options
  • Business Background Limitations: Limited IT understanding causing communication difficulties with developers
  • Underutilized Expertise: Organizations rejecting valuable BA advice due to lack of role understanding

Origins of Business Analysis

Historical Context

  • IT enabled improved business operations and decision-making
  • Shift from operational focus to new services and products
  • Technology enabled new business models and global operations

Current Challenges

  • Growing dissatisfaction with IT support
  • IT investment often fails to deliver required business benefit
  • Poor communication between business and technical experts

Key Development Drivers

1. Impact of Outsourcing

Need for better communication between business and external development teams, especially with geographical distance

2. Competitive Advantage Through IT

Three critical factors: business needs drive IT development, implementation accompanied by business changes, rigorous requirements definition

3. Business Change Programs

Evolution from IT projects to broader business change initiatives requiring specialized roles and skills

Business Analysts as Internal Consultants

Advantages over External Consultants

  • Cost: Lower daily rates
  • Speed: No learning curve about the organization
  • Knowledge Retention: Skills remain within organization
  • Accountability: Must live with impact of recommendations

Business Domain Expertise

  • Deep understanding of organizational context
  • Long-term investment in solutions
  • Continuous relationship building
  • Sustained value delivery

The Range of Analysis Activities

Three Overlapping Areas

Business Analysis bridges the gap between high-level strategy and detailed IT implementation

Strategic Analysis & Definition

  • Primary Responsibility: Senior management and strategy consultants
  • BA Role: Supporting strategic activities rather than leading them
  • Key Requirements for BAs:
    • Access to business strategy information
    • Understanding of strategic context
    • Ability to design tactics that deliver business objectives
    • Expertise in technology opportunities for business improvement
  • Focus: Recommend process and IT solutions that support strategy execution

IT Systems Analysis

Traditional Systems Analysis

  • 40+ years of existence
  • Focus on IT system requirements specification
  • Technical modeling techniques (data, process, function)
  • Detailed software requirements definition

Relationship to Business Analysis

  • Some organizations: Complete separation of BA and IT teams
  • Others: IT Business Analysts vs. "Business" Business Analysts
  • Agile environments: Close collaboration between BAs, users, and developers

Core Business Analysis Activities

  • Localized Business Issues: Resolve specific problems and achieve targeted benefits
  • Broad Business Investigations: Extensive analysis of multiple issues for increased efficiency/effectiveness
  • IT System Enhancement: Focus on replacing/enhancing systems based on business requirements
  • Cross-functional Value Delivery: Senior BA work across workstreams with extensive stakeholder management

Common Starting Point: Gaining understanding of the business situation, clarifying problems, and investigating root causes rather than symptoms

Core Business Analyst Role

Official Definition

"An advisory role which has the responsibility for investigating and analysing business situations, identifying and evaluating options for improving business systems, elaborating and defining requirements, and ensuring the effective implementation and use of information systems in line with the needs of the business."

Core Responsibilities

  • Investigate business systems with holistic view
  • Evaluate actions to improve business system operation
  • Document business requirements for IT system support
  • Elaborate requirements during evolutionary system development

The Rationale for Business Analysis

  • Root Causes Not Symptoms: Investigate root causes, consider holistic view
  • Business Improvement Not IT Change: IT enables opportunities, analyze business improvement, enable agility
  • Options Not Solutions: Challenge predetermined solutions, identify and evaluate options
  • Feasible Contributing Requirements: Consider constraints, evaluate against business needs
  • Entire Business Change Lifecycle: From analysis through post-implementation review
  • Negotiation Not Avoidance: Recognize and negotiate conflicting stakeholder views
